### LOCATION GRADE: C+

**Micro-Area:** Boyle Heights (Hollenbeck Division), East of DTLA

**Justification:** Strong transit access and DTLA adjacency are offset by elevated crime rates and below-average median incomes, placing this location in the upper-average tier for LA County multifamily investment.

**Key Positive Factors:**
- Walk Score 84 ("Very Walkable"), 7-minute walk to Metro E Line (Mariachi Plaza Station), direct one-seat ride to Santa Monica and Long Beach
- Immediate DTLA adjacency (1.5 miles to Arts District, 2 miles to Financial District), proximity to LAC+USC Medical Center (1.4 mi), a major employment anchor with 9,600+ workers
- Active TOD investment corridor: Metro-sponsored joint developments at Mariachi Plaza and Soto stations, affordable housing pipeline, Metrolink station study at LA General Medical Center
- Rent growth of +3.13% YoY in Boyle Heights, with average rents at $2,335/mo (RentCafe, Dec 2025)

**Key Negative Factors:**
- Violent crime grade of D+ (CrimeGrade.org), though Hollenbeck Division homicides dropped 65% in 2024 (40 to 14)
- Median household income $56,623 (below LA City average), limiting tenant purchasing power
- River Implementation Overlay (RIO) and Clean Up Green Up (CUGU) overlays add regulatory layers
- Freeway-adjacent location (I-5, I-10, I-101 convergence) raises air quality and noise concerns

**Comparable Nearby Areas:**
- Lincoln Heights: C+ (similar demographics, improving transit, slightly lower density)
- East Hollywood: B- (higher incomes, stronger retail corridor, more gentrification momentum)
- Historic South Central: C (lower transit scores, similar income levels, weaker rent growth)

**OM Location Paragraph:**
Situated in the heart of Boyle Heights, one of Los Angeles' most dynamic emerging neighborhoods, the property benefits from exceptional connectivity to Downtown Los Angeles, just 1.5 miles from the thriving Arts District across the LA River. Residents enjoy a Walk Score of 84 ("Very Walkable") and a seven-minute walk to the Metro E Line at Mariachi Plaza Station, providing a direct one-seat ride to Santa Monica, Long Beach, and points throughout the Metro system. The neighborhood is anchored by LAC+USC Medical Center, one of the largest public hospital campuses in the nation, employing over 9,600 healthcare professionals within 1.4 miles of the property. LA Metro's continued investment in transit-oriented development at Mariachi Plaza and Soto stations, combined with a proposed Metrolink station at LA General Medical Center, signals sustained public infrastructure commitment to the area. Boyle Heights has seen meaningful rent growth of over 3% year-over-year, driven by limited new supply and increasing demand from DTLA spillover. The neighborhood's rich cultural fabric, walkable streetscape, and proximity to multiple freeways (I-5, I-10, I-101) make it an increasingly compelling submarket for multifamily investment.
